# FIFA World Cup 2026 Group F Predictions

## Market Odds vs Model-X Forecasts

**Published Before Kickoff — June 11, 2026**

The FIFA World Cup 2026 will be the largest tournament in football history, featuring 48 nations competing across the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

Before the tournament begins, we are publishing a complete series of group-stage forecasts comparing two independent prediction sources:

1. **Market Consensus** — average odds from FanDuel, DraftKings, BetMGM, OddsChecker, and Bet365.
2. **Model-X** — an AI-powered football forecasting model trained on historical international football data and Transfermarkt-style squad valuation information.

The purpose of this series is simple: publish forecasts before kickoff and revisit them after the group stage to evaluate both the betting market and Model-X against reality.

This article focuses on **Group F**.

---

# Group F Overview

### Teams

* 🇳🇱 Netherlands
* 🇯🇵 Japan
* 🇸🇪 Sweden
* 🇹🇳 Tunisia

At first glance, Group F appears straightforward.

The Netherlands enter as clear favorites and are expected to top the group. However, the race for second place is where the story becomes interesting.

The betting market expects Japan to comfortably advance alongside the Netherlands. Model-X disagrees.

While both forecasting systems project the Dutch to win the group, Model-X predicts Sweden—not Japan—to claim the second qualification spot.

As a result, Group F becomes one of the strongest blind-test opportunities in the entire tournament.
