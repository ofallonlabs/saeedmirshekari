# FIFA World Cup 2026 Group D Predictions

## Market Odds vs Model-X Forecasts

**Published Before Kickoff — June 11, 2026**

The FIFA World Cup 2026 will be the largest World Cup ever held, jointly hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

Before a single match is played, we are publishing a complete set of group-stage forecasts using two independent prediction sources:

1. **Market Consensus** — the average odds from FanDuel, DraftKings, BetMGM, OddsChecker, and Bet365.
2. **Model-X** — an AI-driven football forecasting model built using historical international match data and Transfermarkt-style squad valuation information.

The objective is not to explain results after they happen. The objective is to publish expectations before kickoff and then evaluate both the market and Model-X after the group stage is complete.

Group D is one of the most interesting groups in the tournament because both forecasting systems arrive at the exact same final standings despite using entirely different approaches.

# Group D Overview

### Teams

* 🇺🇸 United States
* 🇵🇾 Paraguay
* 🇦🇺 Australia
* 🇹🇷 Türkiye

Group D contains two teams expected to compete for first place and two teams attempting to disrupt the qualification race.

The United States enters as one of the host nations and benefits from home support. Türkiye arrives with one of the strongest squads in the group and is viewed favorably by both forecasting systems.

Paraguay appears competitive but sits a tier below the top two. Australia enters as the group's underdog.

Unlike Group A, where Market and Model-X disagreed on most matches, Group D produces remarkable consensus.

# Final Thoughts

Group D is one of the cleanest forecasting groups in the tournament.

The market and Model-X agree on all six match winners, the final standings, and the two qualifying teams. That level of agreement is rare and makes Group D a useful benchmark for evaluating both systems.

If the group unfolds as expected, both forecasting approaches gain credibility. If unexpected results emerge, Group D may become one of the best examples of how difficult World Cup prediction remains, even when consensus appears strong.
